Career Journey

My interest in physics and mechanics helped me chose Mechanical Engineering as my undergraduate major. For most part of my undergraduate, I worked on Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), and Numerical Methods. In my final semester I focused on implementing the CFD theory into miniature C and MATLAB programs. I started to enjoy programming these methods instead of using well-developed software that does the same function. Post graduation, I worked on engineering and designing boilers and its ancillary equipment. During this work experience, I found myself to be more inclined towards programming and computer science. So, I equipped myself with foundational knowledge in computer science through online courses (MOOCS) and projects, and worked as a Software Engineer for 9 months at SenecaGlobal. Being a fresh graduate, I was provided with training on objected-oriented analysis and design, professional software engineering practices including coding standards, code reviews, testing, and software development life cycle. Later, I worked on projects involving both Front-end and backend technologies. The learnings from these projects motivated me to explore computer science further. In Fall 2021, I started pursuing a master of science in computer science at Texas A&M University. I am actively looking for internship opportunities in Software Engineering for Summer 2022.
